A

axioms

Simplicity is not the absence of complexity, but its resolution.
Corollary: A system that hides its complexity is user-friendly; a system that resolves it is elegant.
If it cannot be measured, it cannot be improved. If it cannot be defined, it cannot be measured.
Most engineering failures are semantic failures disguised as technical ones. We build the wrong thing because we defined the problem in words that allowed for ambiguity. Code does not forgive ambiguity.
$\lim_{t \to \infty} P(\text{entropy}) = 1$
Systems naturally degrade. Maintenance is not a chore; it is the act of fighting thermodynamics.
The tool shapes the hand.
We tend to think we choose our tools based on the problem. In reality, the problem is often reframed to fit the tools we know. To think new thoughts, one must occasionally build new tools.
Beauty is a signal of truth.
Not all true things are beautiful, but in mathematics and code, ugliness is almost always a sign of error.
